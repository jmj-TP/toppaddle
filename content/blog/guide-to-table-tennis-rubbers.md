<p>When players talk about upgrading their racket, they often focus on the blade. But the truth is, your rubbers influence your performance more than almost anything else. They define how much spin you can create, how fast the ball leaves your racket, and how much control you have during rallies.</p>

<p>There are several different types of rubbers, each made for a specific style of play. Tacky rubbers, such as the DHS Hurricane series, are sticky to the touch. They grip the ball longer and allow for extremely high spin potential. This is why you often see them on the rackets of top Chinese players.</p>

<p>European-style tensor rubbers, like Butterfly Tenergy or Donic Bluefire, feel bouncier and generate faster shots with less effort. They are great for aggressive topspin play. Then there are pips-out rubbers, which have small bumps instead of a smooth surface. These are often used by defensive players who block, chop, or disrupt their opponent's rhythm.</p>

<p>The sponge underneath the rubber is just as important. A thicker sponge (2.0 mm or more) makes the racket faster and more powerful, while a thinner sponge gives more control. If you are an allround player, you might prefer something around 1.8 mm.</p>

<p>When you combine rubber type and sponge thickness, you get endless possibilities. That is why players experiment so much. If you are not sure where to start, the Yasaka Mark V is a classic, balanced rubber suitable for most skill levels. For advanced loopers, Butterfly <a href="/blog/tenergy-05-vs-dignics-05">Tenergy 05</a> or Donic Bluefire M2 are modern favorites that offer outstanding spin and speed.</p>

<p>To maintain your rubbers and keep them performing at their best, it is important to clean them regularly with proper equipment. A quality cleaning set includes everything you need to keep your rubbers clean and tacky, extending their lifespan and ensuring consistent performance.</p>
