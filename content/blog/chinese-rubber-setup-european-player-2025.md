<h2>Key Differences</h2>
<p><strong>Blade character:</strong> The DHS Power G7 is fast and medium-stiff, amplifying Hurricane 3 Neo's spin well. The Petr Korbel is slower and more controlled — it provides better touch on short game and more stability under pressure.</p>
<p><strong>Backhand feel:</strong> Setup A uses Dynaryz AGR — fast, tensor, direct. Setup B uses Tenergy 05 FX — softer and more consistent. For players learning the Hurricane forehand, Setup B's backhand is significantly more forgiving.</p>
<p><strong>Hurricane 3 Neo notes:</strong> Without boosting, this rubber is slower and heavier than with it. Most club players will find it more than usable unboosted, especially on the G7 which adds pace to compensate.</p>
<p><strong>Control:</strong> Setup B's Petr Korbel gives Control ~77 vs Setup A's ~69. For serves, receives and tight backhand touches, Setup B is noticeably more precise.</p>

<h2>Who Should Build Setup A</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Players who want maximum value from a Chinese-style build under $200</li>
  <li>Intermediate to advanced players with committed, complete strokes</li>
  <li>Anyone exploring Chinese rubber play for the first time</li>
</ul>

<h2>Who Should Build Setup B</h2>
<ul>
  <li>European-style players who want to add a tacky forehand while keeping a familiar backhand</li>
  <li>Players who want more blade control and dwell time</li>
  <li>Anyone who has experimented with Hurricane rubbers before and wants a proven, match-tested build</li>
</ul>



<h2>Verdict</h2>
<p>Setup A is the better value — extraordinary spin potential at $186 makes it the best entry into Chinese rubber play. Setup B is refined and battle-tested — the classic answer to "which blade for Hurricane 3 Neo" for over a decade of European club players. If budget is a priority, go A. If you want the more complete, more controllable build — go B.</p>
