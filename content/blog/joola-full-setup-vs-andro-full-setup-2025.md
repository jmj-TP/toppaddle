<h2>Key Differences</h2>
<p><strong>Blade speed:</strong> The Vyzaryz Freeze (Speed 90) is faster than the Treiber CI (Speed 87). On the Freeze the ball leaves with more pace and less dwell time — rewarding players who generate power from body rotation.</p>
<p><strong>Backhand feel:</strong> Dynaryz CMD is faster but slightly harder to control than Rasanter R42. The R42's lower sponge hardness makes it noticeably more forgiving under pressure — excellent for consistent backhand blocks and drives.</p>
<p><strong>Forehand spin:</strong> Both forehand rubbers (Dynaryz AGR and Rasanter R47) score 91-93 on spin. The difference comes down to individual feel preference rather than objective performance.</p>
<p><strong>Price:</strong> Setup B saves $100. The Treiber CI is meaningfully cheaper than the Vyzaryz Freeze, and the Rasanter series is competitively priced. Andro gives more per dollar.</p>
<p><strong>Asymmetric BH speed:</strong> The Andro setup runs a faster forehand (R47) and more controlled backhand (R42) — a popular choice for players who want explosive FH attacks with a reliable BH game.</p>

<h2>Who Should Build the JOOLA Setup</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Players who prioritise maximum speed above all else</li>
  <li>Big hitters who generate power from strong body mechanics</li>
  <li>Players who want a unified rubber feel across both wings</li>
</ul>

<h2>Who Should Build the Andro Setup</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Players who want high performance at better value</li>
  <li>Intermediate to advanced players who want controlled aggression rather than raw power</li>
  <li>Players who specifically want different FH and BH rubber characteristics</li>
  <li>Anyone from an all-wood blade moving to carbon without losing consistency</li>
</ul>



<h2>Verdict</h2>
<p>The Andro setup wins on value — $100 cheaper with only marginal speed reduction and better backhand consistency. The JOOLA setup is a premium power option — outstanding, but demanding. If building your first custom carbon setup, the Andro combination is the smarter investment. Both will noticeably outperform most pre-assembled rackets at their price point.
