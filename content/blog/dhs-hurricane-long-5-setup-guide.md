<p><strong>TL;DR:</strong> The <a href="/product/blade/dhs-hurricane-long-5">DHS Hurricane Long 5</a> is an Innerforce carbon blade that provides massive dwell time. Paired with <a href="/product/rubber/dhs-hurricane-3-neo">DHS Hurricane 3 Neo</a> on the forehand and <a href="/product/rubber/nittaku-fastarc-g-1">Nittaku Fastarc G-1</a> on the backhand, this is the ultimate Sino-European hybrid setup. Try building it in our <a href="/configurator">Racket Builder</a>.</p>

<h2>The Core: DHS Hurricane Long 5</h2>
<p>Used famously by Ma Long, the <a href="/product/blade/dhs-hurricane-long-5">Hurricane Long 5</a> places its ALC carbon core next to the center ply rather than the outer plies. This "Inner-Carbon" structure means the blade feels like a pure 5-ply wood blade during slow shots, but activates blistering carbon speed when you hit hard.</p>

<h2>Forehand: DHS Hurricane 3 Neo</h2>
<p>A true Chinese tacky rubber, the <a href="/product/rubber/dhs-hurricane-3-neo">Hurricane 3 Neo</a> is arguably the spinniest rubber in the world when driven hard. Because the Long 5 blade's Limba outer plies hold the ball, the sticky surface of the Hurricane 3 is given maximum time to brush the ball into a massive loop.</p>

<h2>Backhand: Nittaku Fastarc G-1</h2>
<p>While Chinese rubbers are great for forehands, backhands usually require easier speed generation. The <a href="/product/rubber/nittaku-fastarc-g-1">Nittaku Fastarc G-1</a> is an outstanding German-made Tensor rubber. It provides a direct, punchy trajectory that perfectly balances the spin-heavy forehand.</p>
