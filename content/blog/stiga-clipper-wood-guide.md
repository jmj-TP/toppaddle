<h2>Why Choose a 7-Ply Wood Blade Over Carbon?</h2>
<p>In an era dominated by Arylate-Carbon (ALC) and Zylon-Carbon (ZLC) blades, 7-ply all-wood blades like the <a href="/product/blade/stiga-clipper-wood">Clipper</a> hold a special place. Carbon provides a massive sweet spot and tremendous speed, but often at the cost of "feeling"—that vital vibration feedback you get when striking the ball. The <a href="/product/blade/stiga-clipper-wood">Clipper</a> utilizes 7 distinct layers of wood to achieve a stiffness and speed comparable to carbon, while retaining a pure, linear, wooden touch.</p>

<p>When you hit the ball gently with a <a href="/product/blade/stiga-clipper-wood">Clipper</a>, it responds gently (excellent control). When you hit hard, it is rock solid and delivers massive power. Many advanced players prefer this linear response over the "bouncy" catapult effect found in many carbon blades.</p>

<h2>The Undisputed King of Short Pimples</h2>
<p>If you ask any high-level short pips player what blade they recommend, the <a href="/product/blade/stiga-clipper-wood">Stiga Clipper</a> is almost always the immediate answer. Why? Short pimples require a stiff, rigid blade to execute fast punches, flat hits, and crisp blocks effectively. A flexible blade will cause the ball to sink in and pop up, destroying the deceptive flatness of a short-pip shot. The <a href="/product/blade/stiga-clipper-wood">Clipper’s</a> 7-ply stiffness makes it the perfect vehicle for this aggressive style.</p>

<h2>Who Should Choose the Stiga Clipper Wood</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Short Pips Attackers:</strong> As mentioned, it is the holy grail for flat hitting and blocking.</li>
  <li><strong>Players Developing Their Strokes:</strong> If you are moving from an all-round 5-ply blade (like a Stiga Allround Classic) to a faster setup, the Clipper forces you to use proper technique to generate spin without the artificial assistance of carbon.</li>
  <li><strong>Purists:</strong> Players who simply prefer the unfiltered feedback of an all-wood blade but still need the speed required for the 40+ plastic ball.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Verdict</h2>
<p>The Stiga Clipper Wood is a timeless classic for a reason. At roughly half the price of modern high-end carbon blades, it offers professional-level performance, unmatched linear feel, and is simply the best all-wood offensive blade available for direct attackers.</p>
