<p>When players transition from their first custom table tennis racket to an intermediate or advanced setup, the most common question revolves around the backhand wing. Finding a rubber that provides the necessary "bite" for opening loops against backspin, while remaining stable for punch blocks, is difficult. Two names constantly appear in this discussion: the <strong><a href="/product/rubber/yasaka-rakza-7">Yasaka Rakza 7</a></strong> and the <strong><a href="/product/rubber/tibhar-evolution-mx-p">Tibhar Evolution MX-P</a></strong>.</p>

<h2>Yasaka Rakza 7: Maximum Grip, Ultimate Forgiveness</h2>
<p>The <a href="/product/rubber/yasaka-rakza-7">Yasaka Rakza 7</a> utilizes natural rubber in its topsheet. This provides an extraordinary amount of grip, making it a "forgiving" tensor rubber. For the backhand, this means unparalleled safety when opening loops. If you mistime a backhand flick over the table or brush a backspin ball a little too thinly, the Rakza 7 still manages to grip the plastic and throw the ball over the net.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Speed:</strong> Fast enough for finishing strokes, but very controllable in the short game.</li>
  <li><strong>Spin:</strong> Top-tier, highly mechanical grip that is easy to access.</li>
  <li><strong>Best For:</strong> Developing loopers, consistent counter-attackers, and players improving their backhand flick.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Tibhar Evolution MX-P: Explosive, Direct Power</h2>
<p>If Rakza 7 is a precision scalpel, the <a href="/product/rubber/tibhar-evolution-mx-p">Tibhar Evolution MX-P</a> is a hammer. Widely considered one of the most powerful ESN (German-made tensor) rubbers on the market, the MX-P features a highly porous, large-pored red sponge. The ball penetrates deeply into the sponge upon contact, launching out with a tremendous catapult effect.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Speed:</strong> Elite-level speed. Unforgiving if your technique is passive.</li>
  <li><strong>Spin:</strong> Excellent, but requires faster arm speed to engage the stiff topsheet.</li>
  <li><strong>Best For:</strong> Advanced players who rely on aggressive, flat backhand punch blocks and mid-distance counter-looping.</li>
</ul>

<h2>The Verdict: Which is Right for You?</h2>
<p>If you play close to the table, rely on heavy spin opens, and prioritize safety over speed on your backhand, <strong>choose the Rakza 7</strong>.</p>

<p>If you have an aggressive, developed backhand, stand slightly further off the table, and want to win the point outright with flat counter-drives, <strong>choose the Evolution MX-P</strong>.</p>
